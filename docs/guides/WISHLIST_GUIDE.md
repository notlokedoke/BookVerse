## What the Wishlist Does

The wishlist is your personal list of books you want to acquire through trading. It serves three key purposes:

1. **Automatic matching** — BookVerse continuously checks all active book listings against your wishlist and identifies matches using intelligent matching algorithms
2. **Social discovery** — Your wishlist appears on your public profile, so traders who own those books can see what you're looking for and proactively offer trades
3. **Trading coordination** — By showing both what you have (your listings) and what you want (your wishlist), your profile gives other traders a complete picture for identifying mutually beneficial trades

## Adding Books to Your Wishlist

You can add books to your wishlist in two ways: directly from any book listing page, or by creating a new wishlist entry manually.

### Method 1: Add from a Book Listing

The quickest way to add a book is directly from any book listing page when you find a book you're interested in.

**Step 1** — Browse books or search for a specific title on the Browse page.

**Step 2** — Click on any book to view its full details.

**Step 3** — On the book detail page, click the **Add to Wishlist** button.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  To Kill a Mockingbird                   │
│  Harper Lee · Good condition             │
│                                          │
│  [ Propose Trade ]  [ Add to Wishlist ]  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Step 4** — A success notification confirms the book has been added to your wishlist.

**Benefits of this method:**
- The book's title, author, and ISBN are captured automatically
- No manual typing required
- Ensures accurate information from the listing

### Method 2: Add Manually

Use the manual form to add a book that isn't currently listed on BookVerse — for example, a book you've been searching for that no one has listed yet.

**Step 1** — Go to **My Wishlist** in the navigation bar.

**Step 2** — Click **+ Add Book** or the floating action button.

**Step 3** — Fill in the wishlist form:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Add Book to Wishlist                        │

│  └────────────────────────────────────────┘  │
│                                              │
│  Author                                      │
│  ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐  │
│  │ George Orwell                          │  │
│  └────────────────────────────────────────┘  │
│                                              │
│  ISBN (optional)                             │
│  ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐  │
│  │ 9780451524935                          │  │
│  └────────────────────────────────────────┘  │
│  Helps with accurate matching              │
│                                              │
│  Notes (optional)                            │
│  ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐  │
│  │ Prefer Signet Classic edition.         │  │
│  │ Any condition Good or above.           │  │
│  └────────────────────────────────────────┘  │
│  Add any specific preferences for trades.    │
│                                              │

└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Required fields:**
- **Title** — The book's title (required)

**Optional fields:**
- **Author** — The author's name (recommended for better matching)
- **ISBN** — The book's ISBN-10 or ISBN-13 (highly recommended for exact matching)
- **Notes** — Any specific preferences, such as edition, condition, or other details

**Step 4** — Click **Add to Wishlist**. A success notification confirms the save and the book appears in your wishlist.

### Tips for Adding Books

**Always include the ISBN when possible**
- ISBN enables the most accurate matching (Perfect Match level)
- Even if you don't know the ISBN, you can look it up on the back cover or copyright page of any copy of the book
- Both ISBN-10 and ISBN-13 formats are accepted

**Be specific in your notes**
- Mention preferred editions (e.g., "First edition" or "Penguin Classics")
- Specify minimum condition requirements (e.g., "Good condition or better")
- Note any special features you're looking for (e.g., "With dust jacket" or "Illustrated edition")

**Include the author name**
- Helps distinguish between books with similar titles
- Enables Strong Match level matching (Title + Author)
- Especially important for common titles like "The Odyssey" or "Hamlet"

### Editing Wishlist Items

To update the notes or other details for a wishlist item:

1. Click **View Details** on the item
2. Click **Edit** on the details page
3. Update the information
4. Click **Save Changes**

You can update:
- Notes field
- Author name (if it was missing or incorrect)
- ISBN (if you found it after initially adding the book)

The title cannot be edited — if you need to change the title, remove the item and add it again with the correct title.

### Duplicate Prevention

BookVerse prevents duplicate wishlist entries. If you try to add a book that's already in your wishlist:
- You'll see a notification: "This book is already in your wishlist"
- The existing entry remains unchanged
- No duplicate is created

Duplicates are detected by:
- Exact ISBN match (if both entries have ISBNs)
- Exact title match (case-insensitive)

## How Wishlist Matching Works

BookVerse automatically checks all active book listings against your wishlist items and identifies matches using a three-level matching system. The system runs continuously, so new matches appear as soon as books are listed.

### The Three Match Levels

BookVerse uses three progressively broader matching strategies to find books that correspond to your wishlist items. Each level has a different match quality score.

#### Level 1: Perfect Match (100% — ISBN Exact Match)

**How it works:** If your wishlist item has an ISBN, BookVerse searches for listings with the identical ISBN.

**Why it's reliable:** An ISBN uniquely identifies a specific edition of a book. An ISBN match guarantees you're looking at the exact edition you want.

**Example:**
```
Your wishlist:  1984 · ISBN 9780451524935
Match found:    1984 · ISBN 9780451524935  ✓ Perfect Match (100%)
```

**Match badge:** 🎯 **Perfect Match**

#### Level 2: Strong Match (90% — Title + Author Exact Match)

**How it works:** If no ISBN match is found (or if your wishlist item doesn't have an ISBN), BookVerse looks for listings where both the title and author match exactly (case-insensitive).

**Why it's useful:** This catches the same book across different editions that may have different ISBNs. For example, a paperback and hardcover edition of the same book will match.

**Example:**
```
Your wishlist:  1984 by George Orwell
Match found:    1984 by George Orwell  ✓ Strong Match (90%)
```

**Match badge:** ⭐ **Strong Match**

#### Level 3: Fuzzy Match (60-89% — Similar Title)

**How it works:** If neither of the above produces a result, BookVerse performs a fuzzy title search using Levenshtein distance (edit distance) to measure similarity. Only results scoring 60% or above are shown.

**Why it's useful:** This catches listings where the title has minor variations, such as:
- Subtitle differences (*1984* vs *1984: A Novel*)
- Punctuation differences (*The Lord of the Rings* vs *Lord of the Rings*)
- Minor spelling variations or formatting

**Example:**
```
Your wishlist:  1984
Match found:    George Orwell's 1984  ✓ Fuzzy Match (75%)
```

**Match badge:** 🔍 **Fuzzy Match (75%)**

### Match Quality Summary

| Match Level | Method | Score | Badge | Reliability |
|-------------|--------|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Perfect Match** | ISBN exact match | 100% | 🎯 Perfect Match | Highest — guaranteed same edition |
| **Strong Match** | Title + Author exact | 90% | ⭐ Strong Match | High — same book, possibly different edition |
| **Fuzzy Match** | Similar title (Levenshtein) | 60-89% | 🔍 Fuzzy Match (%) | Moderate — review carefully |

### What Gets Excluded from Matches

BookVerse automatically excludes:
- **Your own listings** — You won't see your own books as matches for your wishlist
- **Unavailable books** — Books marked as unavailable or involved in active trades are excluded
- **Low-quality fuzzy matches** — Titles with less than 60% similarity are not shown

### How Matching Improves Your Results

**With ISBN:**
```
Your wishlist: 1984 · ISBN 9780451524935
Results:       Perfect Match (100%) → 1 exact edition found
```

**With Title + Author (no ISBN):**
```
Your wishlist: 1984 by George Orwell
Results:       Strong Match (90%) → 3 editions found
               Fuzzy Match (75%) → 2 similar titles found
```

**With Title only (no author, no ISBN):**
```
Your wishlist: 1984
Results:       Fuzzy Match (60-89%) → 8 similar titles found
               (May include unrelated books like "1984 Recipes")
```

**Recommendation:** Always include ISBN when possible for the most accurate matches. If ISBN is not available, include the author name to improve match quality.

### Understanding the Match Display

Each match card shows:

**Book Information:**
- Cover image (if available)
- Title and author
- Condition rating
- Location where the book is listed

**Match Quality Badge:**
- 🎯 **Perfect Match (100%)** — ISBN exact match
- ⭐ **Strong Match (90%)** — Title + Author exact match
- 🔍 **Fuzzy Match (%)** — Similar title with similarity percentage

**Lister Information:**
- Username of the person who listed the book
- Their location (if they've made it public)

**Actions:**
- **View Listing** — See the full book details, photos, and description
- **Propose Trade** — Initiate a trade offer for this book

### Match Sorting

Matches are sorted by quality:
1. Perfect Matches (100%) appear first
2. Strong Matches (90%) appear second
3. Fuzzy Matches (60-89%) appear last, sorted by similarity score

Within each quality level, matches are sorted by most recently listed.

### Email Notifications

If **Wishlist Matches** email notifications are enabled in **Settings → Notifications**, BookVerse will email you when a new match becomes available.

**What triggers an email:**
- A new book is listed that matches one of your wishlist items
- The match quality is Strong Match (90%) or Perfect Match (100%)
- Fuzzy Matches do not trigger email notifications (to avoid false positives)

**Email content includes:**
- The matched book's title and author
- Match quality level
- Link to view the listing
- Link to propose a trade

**Managing notifications:**
1. Go to **Settings → Notifications**
2. Toggle **Wishlist Matches** on or off
3. Click **Save Changes**

Wishlist match notifications are enabled by default. You can turn them off if you prefer to check matches manually on your Dashboard.

## How the Wishlist Helps with Trading

The wishlist is a powerful tool that makes finding and completing trades easier and more efficient. Here's how it helps you trade successfully on BookVerse.

### 1. Passive Discovery — Let Books Find You

Once a book is on your wishlist, you don't need to keep searching for it manually. BookVerse monitors all new listings automatically and alerts you when a match appears.

**Without wishlist:**
```
You → Search "1984" every few days
   → Check results manually
   → Hope someone listed it since last check
```

**With wishlist:**
```
You → Add "1984" to wishlist once
BookVerse → Monitors all new listings automatically
         → Alerts you immediately when match appears
         → Shows match on Dashboard and via email
```

**Benefits:**
- Save time — no need for repeated manual searches
- Never miss a listing — get notified as soon as a match appears
- Be among the first to propose a trade when a new listing goes live

### 2. Social Discovery — Let Traders Find You

Your wishlist appears on your public profile, making it visible to all BookVerse users. When traders browse your profile, they can see exactly what you're looking for.

**How it works:**
```
Trader visits your profile
  ↓
Sees your wishlist: "1984 by George Orwell"
  ↓
Realizes they own that book
  ↓
Proposes a trade directly to you
```

**Benefits:**
- Traders can proactively offer books you want
- No need to wait for them to list the book publicly
- Creates opportunities for direct, targeted trades

**Example scenario:**

> Sarah lists *To Kill a Mockingbird* for trade. John visits Sarah's listing and profile to learn more about her. He sees *1984* on her wishlist. John owns *1984* but hasn't listed it yet. He messages Sarah: "I see you want *1984*. I have a copy in Good condition. Would you trade your *Mockingbird* for it?" Sarah accepts, and they complete the trade — all because her wishlist was visible on her profile.

### 3. Complete Trading Picture

The wishlist works alongside your book listings to give other traders a complete view of your trading interests:

**Your profile shows:**
- **Your listings** — Books you have and want to trade away
- **Your wishlist** — Books you want to acquire

This complete picture helps traders identify mutually beneficial trades at a glance.

**Example:**

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  @bookworm42's Profile                   │
├──────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  📚 Listed Books (3)                     │
│  • To Kill a Mockingbird                 │
│  • The Great Gatsby                      │
│  • Pride and Prejudice                   │
│                                          │
│  ♥  Wishlist (2)                         │
│  • 1984                                  │
│  • Dune                                  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

A trader who owns *1984* and wants *The Great Gatsby* can immediately see this is a perfect match and propose the trade.

### 4. Intelligent Matching Reduces False Positives

The three-level matching system (Perfect, Strong, Fuzzy) helps you focus on the most relevant matches:

**Perfect Matches (100%)** — ISBN exact match
- Guaranteed to be the exact edition you want
- No need to verify — you can propose a trade immediately

**Strong Matches (90%)** — Title + Author exact match
- Very likely to be what you want
- Quick verification recommended (check edition/condition)

**Fuzzy Matches (60-89%)** — Similar title
- May require more careful review
- Useful for discovering variations you didn't know about

This tiered approach means you spend less time reviewing irrelevant results and more time on promising matches.

### 5. Coordinated Trading Strategy

Use your wishlist strategically to maximize trading opportunities:

**Strategy 1: List what others want**
- Check other users' public wishlists
- If you own books they're looking for, list them
- You'll appear in their matches immediately

**Strategy 2: Build a diverse wishlist**
- Add multiple books you're interested in
- More wishlist items = more potential matches
- Increases your chances of finding trades

**Strategy 3: Include ISBN for precision**
- Always add ISBN when you want a specific edition
- Perfect Matches (100%) ensure you get exactly what you want
- Reduces back-and-forth about editions

**Strategy 4: Use notes to set expectations**
- Specify minimum condition requirements
- Mention preferred editions
- Helps traders know if their copy meets your needs before proposing

## Best Practices

Follow these best practices to get the most out of your wishlist and increase your chances of successful trades.

### Always Include ISBN When Possible

**Why it matters:**
- Enables Perfect Match (100%) — the most accurate matching level
- Guarantees you get the exact edition you want
- Eliminates confusion about different editions

**How to find ISBN:**
- Check the back cover of any copy of the book
- Look on the copyright page (usually page 2 or 3)
- Search online book databases (Google Books, Open Library)
- Both ISBN-10 and ISBN-13 formats work

**Example:**
```
Without ISBN:  "1984" → 8 fuzzy matches (60-85% similarity)
With ISBN:     "1984 · ISBN 9780451524935" → 1 perfect match (100%)
```

### Include Author Name

**Why it matters:**
- Enables Strong Match (90%) when ISBN isn't available
- Distinguishes between books with similar titles
- Improves match accuracy significantly

**Common titles that need author names:**
- "The Odyssey" (Homer vs. other authors)
- "Hamlet" (Shakespeare vs. adaptations)
- "1984" (George Orwell vs. other books with "1984" in title)

### Write Detailed Notes

**What to include:**
- **Edition preferences:** "Prefer Penguin Classics edition" or "Any edition is fine"
- **Condition requirements:** "Good condition or better" or "Acceptable condition OK"
- **Special features:** "With dust jacket" or "Illustrated edition preferred"
- **Language:** "English translation only" (for translated works)

**Example notes:**
```
Good: "Prefer Signet Classic edition. Good condition or better. 
       No heavy highlighting or writing."

Too vague: "Any condition"
```

### Build a Diverse Wishlist

**Why it matters:**
- More wishlist items = more potential matches
- Increases your chances of finding trades
- Shows traders you're an active participant

**Recommended approach:**
- Add 5-10 books you're actively seeking
- Include a mix of popular and niche titles
- Update regularly as your interests change

### Keep Your Wishlist Current

**Regular maintenance:**
- Remove books you're no longer interested in
- Update notes if your preferences change
- Add new books as you discover them

**After completing a trade:**
- Remove the book from your wishlist (or it will continue matching)
- Add new books to keep your wishlist active

### Review Fuzzy Matches Carefully

**Fuzzy Matches (60-89%) require verification:**
- Check the full title and author
- Verify it's the book you want (not just a similar title)
- Review the edition and condition

**Example of false positive:**
```
Your wishlist: "1984"
Fuzzy Match:   "1984 Recipes: A Cookbook" (70% similarity)
               ↑ Not what you want — title similarity only
```

**How to avoid false positives:**
- Always include author name in your wishlist
- Use ISBN when possible
- Focus on Perfect and Strong Matches first

### Respond Quickly to Matches

**Why speed matters:**
- Multiple users may want the same book
- First to propose a trade has the best chance
- Shows you're an active, engaged trader

**Enable email notifications:**
1. Go to **Settings → Notifications**
2. Enable **Wishlist Matches**
3. Check your email regularly

**When you receive a match notification:**
- Review the listing within 24 hours
- Propose a trade if interested
- Be prepared with books to offer in exchange

### Use Wishlist to Plan Your Listings

**Strategic listing:**
- Check other users' public wishlists
- If you own books they're looking for, list them
- You'll appear in their matches immediately

**Example:**
```
1. Browse profiles of active traders
2. Note books on their wishlists
3. If you own any, list them
4. They receive match notification
5. Higher chance of trade proposal
```

### Combine Wishlist with Active Searching

**Best approach:**
- Use wishlist for passive monitoring
- Also browse and search actively
- Check Wishlist Matches page weekly

**Why both matter:**
- Wishlist catches new listings automatically
- Active searching finds existing listings
- Together, they maximize your trading opportunities

## Troubleshooting

### "This book is already in your wishlist"

BookVerse prevents duplicates. If you see this message, the book is already saved. Visit **My Wishlist** to find it.

### No matches found on the Matches page

- Matches only include books currently listed as available. If no one has listed the book yet, the section will be empty.
- Add more books to your wishlist to increase the chance of a match.
- Check back after a few days — new listings are added regularly.

### Cannot add a book to wishlist

**Possible reasons:**
- You're trying to add your own listed book (not allowed)
- The book is already in your wishlist
- You're not logged in

**Solution:**
- Check if you're logged in
- Visit **My Wishlist** to see if the book is already there
- If it's your own book, you cannot add it to your wishlist

### Wishlist match notifications not arriving

**Check these settings:**
1. Go to **Settings → Notifications**
2. Verify **Wishlist Matches** is enabled
3. Check your email spam/junk folder
4. Verify your email address is correct in **Settings → Account**

**Note:** Only Perfect Matches (100%) and Strong Matches (90%) trigger email notifications. Fuzzy Matches do not send emails to avoid false positives.

### Match quality seems wrong

**Understanding match levels:**
- **Perfect Match (100%)** requires exact ISBN match
- **Strong Match (90%)** requires exact title AND author match
- **Fuzzy Match (60-89%)** uses similarity algorithm

**If a match seems incorrect:**
- Review the book details carefully
- Check if it's a different edition or similar title
- Fuzzy Matches may include books with similar but not identical titles
- Focus on Perfect and Strong Matches for most accurate results

### Cannot remove a book from wishlist

**Possible issues:**
- Network connection problem
- Session expired

**Solution:**
- Refresh the page and try again
- Log out and log back in
- Clear browser cache and try again

### Wishlist not showing on my profile

**Check privacy settings:**
- Wishlists are public by default and appear on all user profiles
- If your wishlist isn't showing, try refreshing the page
- Make sure you have at least one book in your wishlist

**Note:** All wishlist items are publicly visible on your profile to help other traders see what you're looking for.
