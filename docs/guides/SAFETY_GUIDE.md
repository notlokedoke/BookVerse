# Safety Guide

BookVerse is a community-driven peer-to-peer book trading platform. Because trades happen between people who may not know each other, following safe practices matters. This guide covers online safety, in-person meeting practices, book condition verification, community guidelines, and how to report problems.

## Online Safety

### Protect Your Personal Information

BookVerse provides an in-app messaging system for all trade communication. Use it — do not move conversations to personal phone numbers, email, or social media accounts.

**Do not share:**
- Your home address
- Phone number
- Financial account details or payment information
- Social media profile links (unless you are comfortable making them fully public)

### Account Security

| Practice | Why it matters |
|----------|---------------|
| Use a strong, unique password (min 8 characters, one uppercase, one number) | Prevents unauthorised access to your trade history and messages |
| Never share your password with anyone | BookVerse staff will never ask for your password |
| Log out when using shared or public computers | Prevents others from accessing your account |
| Keep your email address current | Required for verification and password recovery |

If you notice activity on your account you did not initiate, change your password immediately via **Settings → Account → Change Password** and contact support.

### Privacy Settings

Control what other users see on your public profile under **Settings → Privacy**:

| Setting | Default | Recommendation |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| Show City | On | Keep on — required for local trading to work well |
| Show Email | Off | Leave off unless you specifically want traders to email you directly |

## Safe In-Person Meetings

Most BookVerse trades end in a face-to-face exchange. In-person meetups are generally safe when a few simple rules are followed.

### Choose a Safe Location

**Good locations:**
- Libraries
- Bookshops
- Coffee shops and cafes
- Shopping centres
- Community centres
- University or school common areas
- Police station public lobbies (available in many areas specifically for this purpose)

**Avoid:**
- Private residences — yours or theirs
- Isolated car parks, parks, or alleys, especially after dark
- Any location suggested by the other party that feels unusual or remote

When possible, choose a location that has CCTV coverage.

### Before You Leave

```
Pre-meeting checklist:
  ☐ Tell a trusted person where you are going
  ☐ Share the other trader's name and the meeting location with them
  ☐ Agree on a check-in time — "I'll message you when I'm done"
  ☐ Charge your phone fully before leaving
  ☐ Save the other trader's BookVerse profile link for reference
```

### Bring Support

- Bring a friend or family member whenever possible, especially for a first exchange with someone you have not met before.
- Even if you go alone, let someone know your plans and when to expect you back.

### During the Exchange

- Meet during daylight hours when possible.
- Inspect the book thoroughly before agreeing the trade is complete — see [Book Condition Verification](#book-condition-verification) below.
- Be courteous and calm regardless of any discrepancy you notice. Most issues are honest mistakes.
- Do not hand over your book until you have received and inspected theirs.
- **Trust your instincts.** If anything feels wrong — the location, the person's behaviour, the situation — you are entitled to leave. Your safety is more important than completing any trade. You do not owe anyone an explanation.

### Postal Exchanges

If you and the other trader are in different cities, coordinate by post:

- Agree in writing via BookVerse messages who ships first, or whether you ship simultaneously.
- Share tracking numbers with each other after dispatching.
- Agree on how to handle a delayed or lost parcel before either party ships.
- Inspect your received book before marking the trade complete.

## Book Condition Verification

Inspect the book before finalising any in-person trade. This protects both parties and prevents disputes.

### What to Check

| Area | What to look for |
|------|-----------------|
| **Front and back cover** | Tears, creases, stains, significant scuffs |
| **Spine** | Cracks, breaks, looseness that could worsen with reading |
| **Pages** | Tears, missing pages, water damage, excessive highlighting or writing |
| **Binding** | Pages securely attached, none loose or falling out |
| **Odour** | Musty, smoke, or other persistent smells not mentioned in the listing |
| **Completeness** | All pages present, maps and appendices intact if the book has them |

### Condition Reference

| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Like New** | Minimal wear, unread or barely read, no marks, tight spine |
| **Good** | Some wear, fully readable, light scuffs or small crease, no writing |
| **Fair** | Noticeable wear, worn spine, yellowed pages, possible minor writing |
| **Poor** | Heavy wear, may have torn pages, heavy markings, or broken spine |

### If the Condition Does Not Match

1. Calmly point out the specific discrepancy to the other trader.
2. Consider whether the difference is acceptable to you — a slight variance is common between honest assessors.
3. If the difference is significant and the other trader refuses to acknowledge it, you are within your rights to decline the exchange.
4. Reflect the experience accurately in your rating after the trade is resolved — this protects future traders.

## Community Guidelines

### Fair Trading Practices

- **Describe books honestly.** Use accurate condition grades and real photos of your copy. Do not use publisher stock images to misrepresent wear.
- **Honor accepted trades.** Once you accept a proposal, follow through. Repeatedly abandoning accepted trades harms your reputation and the community.
- **Communicate promptly.** Respond to messages and trade proposals within 24–48 hours. Long silences after acceptance are disrespectful to the other trader's time.
- **Complete trades in a reasonable timeframe.** Once accepted, arrange the exchange without unnecessary delay.

### Respectful Behaviour

- Treat every community member with courtesy, regardless of outcome. Declining a trade or receiving a lower rating than expected does not justify hostile messages.
- Use appropriate language in all messages and comments.
- Provide honest, factual ratings — see the [Rating Guide](RATING_GUIDE.md) for guidance on writing fair feedback.
- Do not post discriminatory, offensive, or harassing content anywhere on the platform.

### Non-Commercial Use

BookVerse is a personal book trading community, not a commercial marketplace:

- List books you personally own and want to trade — not books acquired solely for resale.
- Do not use the platform for bulk trading or business purposes.
- Focus on genuine reading-interest connections, not transaction volume.

### Prohibited Activities

The following are violations of the Terms of Service and may result in account suspension or permanent removal:

- Misrepresenting book condition to deceive trading partners
- Posting false, misleading, or duplicate listings
- Harassment, threats, or abusive communication directed at any user
- Sharing another user's personal information without their consent
- Attempting to arrange transactions outside the platform to avoid the trade record
- Using the platform if you are under 13 years of age

## Red Flags

Be alert to the following warning signs. If you encounter them, pause and reconsider before proceeding.

### Before the Meeting

| Warning sign | What it may indicate |
|---|---|
| Request to meet at a private residence or isolated location | Safety risk — insist on a public place |
| Pressure to meet immediately without time to make arrangements | Preventing you from thinking clearly or informing others |
| Request for your phone number, home address, or financial details | Phishing or scam attempt |
| Offers that seem unusually generous or "too good to be true" | Potential bait-and-switch |
| Profile with no rating history and very recently created | No community track record — proceed with extra caution |
| Consistently low ratings or negative comments from multiple traders | Pattern of problematic behaviour |

### During or After the Exchange

| Warning sign | What it may indicate |
|---|---|
| Aggressive or threatening behaviour | Leave immediately and report |
| Pressure to complete the exchange before you have inspected the book | Trying to prevent you from noticing a problem |
| Book is dramatically different from the listing photos | Deliberate misrepresentation |
| Requests for payment or money of any kind | BookVerse trades are book-for-book only — no cash should be involved |

### Trust Your Instincts

If something feels wrong at any point — before, during, or after a meeting — act on that feeling. Leave, end the conversation, or report the user. No book is worth your safety or wellbeing.

## Reporting Issues

### How to Report

If you experience safety concerns, inappropriate behaviour, or a Terms of Service violation:

1. Use the **Report** feature on user profiles or trade pages within the app.
2. Contact support through the **Help Center** with:
   - The other user's profile name
   - A clear description of what happened
   - Screenshots of any relevant messages or listings
3. **For immediate physical danger, contact your local emergency services first.** BookVerse support cannot act as a substitute for emergency services.

### What Happens After a Report

All reports are reviewed. Reports involving safety, harassment, or fraud are treated as high priority. Confirmed violations result in warnings, trade restrictions, or permanent account removal depending on severity.

Reporting is confidential — the reported user is not told who filed the report.

## Age and Eligibility

- BookVerse users must be **at least 13 years of age**.
- Users under 18 should use the platform under **parental supervision and guidance**.
- Parents and guardians are encouraged to review trade proposals and meeting arrangements made by minors.
- If you believe a minor is using the platform unsafely, report it through the Help Center.
