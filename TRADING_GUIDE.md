## How Trading Works

BookVerse trades are one-for-one book swaps. You offer one of your listed books in exchange for a book listed by another user.

```
  You                           Other Trader
  ────                          ────────────
  Listed books ──── offer ────▶ Listed books
                                      │
                  accepted/declined ◀─┘
                       │
              coordinate handoff
                       │
              mark complete + rate
```

Every trade involves exactly two parties and two books:

| Role | Who | What they do |
|------|-----|--------------|
| **Proposer** | The user who initiates the trade | Selects a book to request and a book to offer |
| **Receiver** | The user who owns the requested book | Accepts or declines the proposal |

---

## Trade Statuses

A trade moves through four possible statuses.

```
  proposed ──▶ accepted ──▶ completed
      │
      └──▶ declined
```

| Status | Meaning | Who sets it |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **proposed** | Trade proposal sent, waiting for response | Set automatically when proposer submits |
| **accepted** | Receiver agreed to the trade | Receiver |
| **declined** | Receiver declined the proposal | Receiver |
| **completed** | Books have been exchanged | Either party (proposer or receiver) |

Once a trade reaches **declined** or **completed** the status cannot change again.

## Proposing a Trade

### Before You Propose

To propose a trade you need at least one available book listed on BookVerse. If you have no listed books, go to **My Books → Add Book** first.

You can only offer books you own. You cannot request your own books, and both the book you are requesting and the book you are offering must be marked as available.

### How to Propose

**Step 1** — Find a book you want. Browse the **Browse** page, search by title or author, or check your **Wishlist Matches**.

**Step 2** — Open the book's detail page and click **Propose Trade**.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Dune                                        │
│  Frank Herbert · Like New                    │
│  Listed by: Alex · Kathmandu                 │
│                                              │
│  [ Propose Trade ]  [ ♥ Add to Wishlist ]    │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Step 3** — A modal opens showing your available books. Select the book you want to offer.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Propose a Trade                             │
│                                              │
│  You want:  Dune by Frank Herbert            │
│                                              │
│  Choose a book to offer:                     │
│                                              │
│  ○  The Hobbit · J.R.R. Tolkien · Good       │
│  ○  1984 · George Orwell · Like New          │
│  ○  Brave New World · Aldous Huxley · Fair   │
│                                              │
│  [ Cancel ]              [ Send Proposal ]   │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Step 4** — Click **Send Proposal**. The proposal is submitted and the other trader receives a notification.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  ✓ Trade proposal sent!                      │
│    Alex will be notified of your proposal.   │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

The trade now has status **proposed** and appears under **My Trades → Outgoing**.

### What Happens After Sending

- The receiver gets an in-app notification and (if enabled) an email.
- While the trade is **proposed**, both books remain listed as available.
- A proposal cannot be withdrawn or edited after sending. If the receiver does not respond, send them a message.

## Responding to a Trade Proposal

When someone proposes a trade for one of your books, you receive a notification in-app and (if enabled) by email.

### Finding Incoming Proposals

Go to **My Trades** and click the **Incoming** tab.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  My Trades                                   │
│  [ All ] [ Incoming ] [ Outgoing ]           │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  📥 Incoming  ·  1 proposal waiting          │
│                                              │
│  Jordan wants to trade:                      │
│  ┌──────────┐       ┌──────────┐             │
│  │ The      │  for  │ Dune     │             │
│  │ Hobbit   │  ──▶  │          │             │
│  └──────────┘       └──────────┘             │
│  Proposed: 2 hours ago    Status: proposed   │
│                                              │
│  [ Accept ]            [ Decline ]           │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Accepting a Proposal

Review the offered book carefully — check its title, author, and condition in the listing. If you are happy to proceed, click **Accept**.

- The trade status changes to **accepted**.
- The proposer is notified.
- If the requested book matches a wishlist item the proposer has, that wishlist item is automatically marked as fulfilled.

### Declining a Proposal

If the offered book is not what you want, click **Decline**.

- The trade status changes to **declined**.
- The proposer is notified.
- Both books remain available for other trades.
- A declined trade is final and cannot be reactivated.

### Things to Consider Before Responding

- Check the **condition** of the offered book in its listing photos and description.
- Look at the **proposer's profile** to see their star rating and completed trade count.
- Respond promptly — leaving proposals unanswered for days reflects poorly in the community.

## After Acceptance — Coordinating the Handoff

Once a trade is **accepted**, both parties arrange the physical exchange of books. BookVerse does not handle logistics — coordination happens between the two traders via the in-app messaging feature.

### Contact the Other Trader

Open the trade detail and use the message link to start a conversation. Confirm details before any books change hands.

### Exchange Methods

| Method | Best for |
|--------|---------|
| **In-person meetup** | Same city — fast, free, lets you inspect the book on the spot |
| **Postal / courier** | Different cities — agree who ships first or ship simultaneously |
| **Drop point** | Agreed neutral location (café, library, university, etc.) |

### Things to Agree in Writing

Before exchanging books, confirm the following by message:

- Exchange method (meetup, post, drop point)
- Date, time, and location if meeting in person
- Who covers shipping if posting (each pays their own, or split)
- Whether tracking is required
- How to handle a book that arrives in worse condition than listed

## Marking a Trade Complete

After both books have been exchanged, either the proposer or the receiver can mark the trade complete.

**Step 1** — Go to **My Trades** and open the accepted trade.

**Step 2** — Click **Mark as Complete**.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Trade with Jordan             Status: accepted│
│                                              │
│  You exchanged:                              │
│  The Hobbit  ◀────────────────▶  Dune        │
│                                              │
│  [ Mark as Complete ]                        │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Step 3** — The trade status changes to **completed**:

- Both books are automatically marked as no longer available.
- Rating is unlocked for both parties.
- The other trader receives a notification.

### When to Mark Complete

Mark the trade complete only after you have physically received the book and are satisfied with its condition. Do not mark complete before the exchange — this action cannot be undone.

## Rating Your Trading Partner

After a trade is marked **completed**, both parties can rate each other. Ratings build the trust reputation displayed on every user's profile.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Rate Your Trading Partner                   │
│  Jordan                                      │
│                                              │
│  ★ ★ ★ ★ ★                                  │
│  1   2   3   4   5 stars                     │
│                                              │
│  [ Submit Rating ]                           │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- Ratings are 1 to 5 stars.
- Each completed trade unlocks one rating per party.
- Ratings are averaged and shown on the trader's public profile.
- Rate honestly — your rating helps the whole community make safe trading decisions.

## Managing Your Trades

Go to **My Trades** in the navigation bar to see all trades you are involved in.

### Tabs

| Tab | Shows |
|-----|-------|
| **All** | Every trade you are part of (proposer or receiver) |
| **Incoming** | Trades where you are the receiver |
| **Outgoing** | Trades where you are the proposer |

### Trade Cards

Each card shows the two books, the other trader's name, when the proposal was made, and a colour-coded status badge.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  📤 Outgoing                                 │
│                                              │
│  ┌──────┐       ┌──────┐                    │
│  │ The  │  ──▶  │ Dune │   ● proposed       │
│  │Hobbit│       │      │                    │
│  └──────┘       └──────┘                    │
│  Proposed: 3 days ago                        │
│                                              │
│  [ View Details ]                            │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Click **View Details** to open the full trade view with all available actions for that status.

## Communication Best Practices

### Respond Quickly

Aim to respond to incoming proposals within 24–48 hours. Traders who routinely leave proposals unanswered develop a poor reputation.

### Be Honest About Condition

The book you offer must match the condition stated in your listing. If the book's condition has changed since you listed it, update the listing or decline any incoming trades until it is corrected.

### Confirm Before You Ship

If posting books, always confirm the plan in writing via BookVerse messages before shipping. Agree on:

- Whether both parties ship simultaneously or one ships first
- Tracking numbers to be shared after dispatch
- What happens if a parcel is delayed or lost

### Inspect on Receipt

Check your received book before marking the trade complete. If it differs significantly from the listing:

1. Take photos of the book and any damage.
2. Contact the other trader immediately via messages.
3. Describe the discrepancy clearly with photos.
4. Allow reasonable time for them to respond before deciding next steps.

### Leave an Honest Rating

Rate every completed trade. A 5-star rating for a smooth experience and an honest lower rating for a problem both help the community. Keep observations factual.

### Example Opening Message After Acceptance

```
Hi Jordan,

Glad you accepted! I'm in Kathmandu — happy to meet in person
if that works for you. Are you free Saturday afternoon?

Alternatively I can post on Monday. Let me know which you prefer.

Thanks,
Alex
```

## Troubleshooting

### "You have no books available to offer"

You need at least one available listed book to propose a trade. Go to **My Books → Add Book** to add a listing. If you have listed books but none appear in the modal, they may already be involved in an accepted trade and marked unavailable.

### "Requested book is not available for trade"

The book you tried to request has been marked unavailable — typically because the owner accepted another trade for it. Return to Browse to find a different copy or a similar title.

### "You cannot request your own book"

You attempted to propose a trade for one of your own listings. Only books listed by other users can be requested.

### I accepted a proposal but want to cancel

There is no cancel action for accepted trades. Contact the other trader by message, explain the situation, and agree together not to mark the trade complete. The trade will remain in the accepted state indefinitely if neither party marks it complete.

### The other trader is not responding after acceptance

Send a follow-up message with a clear proposed deadline. If there is still no response after a reasonable time (e.g., one week), do not mark the trade complete. Leave it in the accepted state and reflect the experience in your rating if the exchange eventually happens.

### The book I received does not match the listing

1. Take photos of the discrepancy.
2. Message the other trader immediately with a description and photos.
3. Agree on a resolution — return, partial exchange, or written acknowledgement.
4. Reflect the experience honestly in your rating.

### The trade shows "completed" but I did not mark it

Either party can mark a trade complete. The other trader marked it before you did. Check your received book. If you have not received it yet, contact the other trader via messages immediately.
